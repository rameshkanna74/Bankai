1.ね
ne
isn’t it
“ね” comes at the end of a sentence. In Japanese conversation, you often hear
“...ですね” (desu ne) or “...ますね” (masune). When you end a sentence with
“ね”, it is to confirm something, seek approval or put emphasis on something.
1.あしたの しごとの しりょうですね。
ashitano shigotono shiryoodesune
These are tomorrow's work materials, right?
2.インドから きましたね。
indokara
kimashitane
You came from India, didn't you?
3.キムさんは かんこくじんですね。
kimsanwa kankokujindesune
Miss Kim is Korean, isn't she?

## Let's expand on those examples and incorporate more diverse scenarios!

### Understanding the Nuances of "ne"

Before we dive into more examples, let's briefly recap the versatility of "ne". It's not just about confirmation; it can also convey:

* **Seeking agreement:** "Isn't it...?"
* **Expressing surprise:** "Really?"
* **Softening a request:** "Could you...?"
* **Adding emphasis:** "You know?"

### Diverse Examples with "ne"

Here are some more examples, categorized by different situations and incorporating a wider range of vocabulary:

**1. At a Restaurant**

* **このラーメン、おいしいですね。** (Kono raamen, oishii desu ne.) - This ramen is delicious, isn't it?
* **辛すぎませんか？** (Karasugimasen ka?) - Isn't it too spicy?
* **もう一杯いかがですか？** (Mou ippai ikaga desu ka?) - Would you like another drink? 

**2. At a Party**
* **この音楽、いいですね。** (Kono ongaku, ii desu ne.) - This music is great, isn't it?
* **最近、忙しかったでしょ？** (Saikin, isogashikatta desho?) - You've been busy lately, haven't you? 
* **あの人、誰かわかりますか？** (Ano hito, dare ka wakarimasu ka?) - Do you know who that person is?

**3. At Work**
* **このプロジェクト、大変ですね。** (Kono purojekuto, taihen desu ne.) - This project is tough, isn't it?
* **明日までに終わらせないと。** (Ashita made ni owarasenaito.) - We need to finish this by tomorrow, don't we?
* **プレゼン、うまくいったね。** (Purezen, umaku itta ne.) - The presentation went well, didn't it?

**4. At School**
* **数学の宿題、難しかったね。** (Sugaku no shukudai, muzukashikatta ne.) - The math homework was difficult, wasn't it?
* **来週、テストがあるって聞いたけど。** (Raishuu, tesuto ga aru tte kiita kedo.) - I heard there's a test next week, right?
* **先生、優しいですね。** (Sensei, yasashii desu ne.) - The teacher is kind, isn't he/she?

**5. General Conversation**
* **最近、日本語の勉強、頑張ってますね。** (Saikin, Nihongo no benkyou, ganbatte masu ne.) - You've been working hard on your Japanese lately, haven't you?
* **今日はいい天気ですね。** (Kyou wa ii tenki desu ne.) - It's a nice day today, isn't it?
* **来年は旅行に行きたいね。** (Rainen wa ryokou ni ikitai ne.) - I want to go on a trip next year, don't you?

### Additional Notes

* **"Desu ne"** is more formal than "ne".
* **"Masu ne"** is used after the "masu" form of verbs and is a more polite way to express agreement or seek confirmation.
* **"Desho"** is often used when you are fairly certain of the answer but want to confirm.

2.この/その/あの + N
kono/sono/ano
this/that/that...over there
“この/その/あの”(kono/sono/ano) are demonstrative words used when
referring to a particular noun. In English, they are “this”, “that”, and
“that...over there”.

1.このひとは だれですか
konohitowa daredesuka
Who is this person?

2.あのかばんは きれいです。
anokabanwa kireedesu
That bag is beautiful.

3.そのひとは わたしの ちちです。
sonohitowa watashino chichidesu
That person is my father.

## Let's expand on the usage of "この/その/あの" with more examples and vocabulary!

### Understanding the nuances of "この/その/あの"
* **この (kono):** Used to point to something near the speaker.
* **その (sono):** Used to point to something near the listener.
* **あの (ano):** Used to point to something far from both the speaker and the listener. 

### Examples in Various Situations

**1. At a Store**

* **このりんごは あまいですか？** (Kono ringo wa amai desu ka?) - Is this apple sweet?
* **そのバナナは いくつですか？** (Sono banana wa ikutsu desu ka?) - How many bananas are those?
* **あのケーキは 美味しそうですね。** (Ano keeki wa oishisou desu ne.) - That cake looks delicious.

**2. In a Restaurant**

* **このお寿司は 本物ですね。** (Kono osushi wa honmono desu ne.) - This sushi is authentic, isn't it?
* **そのラーメン、辛そうです。** (Sono raamen, karasou desu.) - That ramen looks spicy.
* **あのテーブル、空いていますか？** (Ano tēburu, aite imasu ka?) - Is that table free?

**3. At Home**

* **この本、面白いですよ。** (Kono hon, omoshiroi desu yo.) - This book is interesting, you know.
* **その花、きれいですね。** (Sono hana, kirei desu ne.) - That flower is beautiful.
* **あの窓、開けてもいいですか？** (Ano mado, akete mo ii desu ka?) - May I open that window?

**4. In a Classroom**

* **この問題、わかりません。** (Kono mondai, wakarimasen.) - I don't understand this problem.
* **その単語、辞書で調べてください。** (Sono tango, jisho de shirabete kudasai.) - Please look up that word in the dictionary.
* **あの先生、優しいですね。** (Ano sensei, yasashii desu ne.) - That teacher is kind, isn't he/she?

**5. General Conversation**

* **この写真、いつ撮ったの？** (Kono shashin, itsu totta no?) - When did you take this picture?
* **そのプレゼント、誰からもらったの？** (Sono purezento, dare kara moratta no?) - Who gave you that present?
* **あのビル、高いですね。** (Ano biru, takai desu ne.) - That building is tall, isn't it?

### Additional Notes

* You can combine "この/その/あの" with other words to create more complex expressions. For example:
    * **この前 (kono mae):** the other day
    * **そのうち (sono uchi):** soon
    * **あの世 (ano yo):** the other world

* When referring to people, you can also use:
    * **こちら (kochira):** this person (referring to oneself)
    * **そちら (sochira):** that person (referring to the listener)
    * **あちら (achira):** that person over there

By understanding the nuances of "この/その/あの" and practicing with various examples, you'll be able to use these demonstrative words confidently in your Japanese conversations.

## Let's delve deeper into the usage of "これ/それ/あれ" with more diverse examples!

### Understanding the nuances of "これ/それ/あれ"
As a quick recap:
* **これ (kore):** Used to point to something near the speaker.
* **それ (sore):** Used to point to something near the listener.
* **あれ (are):** Used to point to something far from both the speaker and the listener.

### Examples in Various Contexts

**1. At a Party**

* **これは おいしいワインですね。** (Kore wa oishii wain desu ne.) - This is a delicious wine, isn't it?
* **あれは 山田さんですか？** (Are wa Yamada-san desu ka?) - Is that Mr./Ms. Yamada over there?
* **それは 私のプレゼントです。** (Sore wa watashi no purezento desu.) - That is my present.

**2. At a Restaurant**

* **これは メニューですか？** (Kore wa menyu desu ka?) - Is this the menu?
* **あれは スシバーですか？** (Are wa sushi baa desu ka?) - Is that the sushi bar?
* **それは 私のオーダーではありません。** (Sore wa watashi no ooda dewa arimasen.) - That's not my order.

**3. In a Classroom**

* **これは 私のノートです。** (Kore wa watashi no nooto desu.) - This is my notebook.
* **それは 先生のペンですか？** (Sore wa sensei no pen desu ka?) - Is that the teacher's pen?
* **あれは 図書館への道です。** (Are wa toshokan e no michi desu.) - That's the way to the library.

**4. General Conversation**

* **これは 珍しい本ですね。** (Kore wa mezurashii hon desu ne.) - This is a rare book, isn't it?
* **それは 私の趣味です。** (Sore wa watashi no shumi desu.) - That's my hobby.
* **あれは 新しいビルですね。** (Are wa atarashii biru desu ne.) - That's a new building, isn't it?

### Combining with other particles and verbs

* **これを使う (kore o tsukau):** to use this
* **それを買う (sore o kau):** to buy that
* **あれを見る (are o miru):** to look at that
* **これは何ですか？ (Kore wa nan desu ka?):** What is this?
* **それはどこですか？ (Sore wa doko desu ka?):** Where is that?

### Additional Notes

* **これ/それ/あれ** can also be used to refer to abstract concepts or ideas. 
  * **これは良い考えですね。** (Kore wa ii kangae desu ne.) - That's a good idea.
* **これ/それ/あれ** can be combined with other particles to create different nuances.
  * **これこそ (kore koso):** this is exactly what...
  * **それどころか (sore dokoro ka):** not only that, but...
