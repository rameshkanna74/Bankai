## Examples with Essential Japanese Vocabulary (Negative Form)

Here are some more examples of Japanese sentences using essential vocabulary in the negative form:

**1. Negating Verbs**

* **私は日本語を話せません。** (Watashi wa nihongo o hanasemasen) - I cannot speak Japanese.
* **彼はまだ来ていません。** (Kare wa mada kite imasen) - He hasn't arrived yet.
* **今日は雨です。** (Kyou wa ame desu) - It is not raining today.
* **私は料理ができません。** (Watashi wa ryori ga dekinai) - I can't cook.
* **この映画は面白くありませんでした。** (Kono eiga wa omoshirokunakatta desu) - This movie was not interesting.

**2. Negating Adjectives**

* **この本は面白くないです。** (Kono hon wa omoshirokunai desu) - This book is not interesting.
* **彼女はきれいではありません。** (Kanojo wa kirei dewa arimasen) - She is not beautiful.
* **この家は高価ではありません。** (Kono ie wa kouka dewa arimasen) - This house is not expensive.
* **今日は暑くありません。** (Kyou wa atsuku arimasen) - It is not hot today.
* **この問題は簡単ではありません。** (Kono mondai wa kantan dewa arimasen) - This problem is not easy.

**3. Negating Existence**

* **机の上には何もありません。** (Tsukue no ue ni wa nani mo arimasen) - There is nothing on the desk.
* **彼は友人がいません。** (Kare wa yuugajin ga imasen) - He has no friends.
* **この町には病院がありません。** (Kono machi ni wa byouin ga arimasen) - There is no hospital in this town.
* **私はお金を持っていません。** (Watashi wa okane o motte imasen) - I don't have any money.
* **彼は日本語の本を持っていません。** (Kare wa nihongo no hon o motte imasen) - He doesn't have a Japanese book.

**4. Negating Past Actions**

* **昨日は雨が降らなかったです。** (Kinou wa ame ga furaなかった desu) - It didn't rain yesterday.
* **彼は宿題をしなかった。** (Kare wa shukudai o shinakatta) - He didn't do his homework.
* **私は電車に乗らなかった。** (Watashi wa densha ni noranakatta) - I didn't take the train.
* **彼らは映画を見なかった。** (Karera wa eiga o minakatta) - They didn't watch the movie.
* **彼女は電話をかけなかった。** (Kanojo wa denwa o kakenaide) - She didn't call.

**5. Negating Future Plans**

* **明日は学校に行きません。** (Ashita wa gakkou ni ikanai desu) - I won't go to school tomorrow.
* **彼は旅行に行かない。** (Kare wa ryokou ni ikanai) - He won't go on a trip.
* **私たちは映画を見ない。** (Watashitachi wa eiga o minai) - We won't watch the movie.
* **彼女は料理をしない。** (Kanojo wa ryori o shinai) - She won't cook.
* **彼らはパーティーに来ない。** (Karera wa paatii ni konai) - They won't come to the party.

**Additional Tips:**

* Use **ありません** (arimasen) for negating the existence of nouns and **です** (desu) for negating verbs.
* Use **じゃない** (janai) or **ではない** (dewanai) for more informal negation of adjectives.
* Use **まだ** (mada) to express that something has not happened yet.
* Use **ない** (nai) with the past tense verb ending (た -ta) to negate past actions.
* Use **ない** (nai) with the volitional verb ending (よう -you) to negate future plans.

## Examples with Essential Japanese Vocabulary (Question Form)

Here are some more examples of Japanese sentences using essential vocabulary in the question form:

**1. Asking About People**

* **あの方はどなたですか？** (Anokata wa donata desu ka?) - Who is that person? (very polite)
* **あなたの名前はなんですか？** (Anata no namae wa nan desu ka?) - What is your name?
* **どこから来ましたか？** (Doko kara kimashita ka?) - Where did you come from?
* **何歳ですか？** (Nansai desu ka?) - How old are you?
* **何の仕事をしてますか？** (Nani no shigoto o shite masu ka?) - What do you do for work?

**2. Asking About Things**

* **これは何ですか？** (Kore wa nan desu ka?) - What is this?
* **いくらですか？** (Ikura desu ka?) - How much is it?
* **どこにありますか？** (Doko ni arimasu ka?) - Where is it?
* **いつですか？** (Itsu desu ka?) - When is it?
* **どうすればいいですか？** (Dou sureba ii desu ka?) - What should I do?

**3. Asking for Opinions**

* **この映画はどうですか？** (Kono eiga wa dou desu ka?) - How is this movie?
* **このレストランはおいしいですか？** (Kono resutoran wa oishii desu ka?) - Is this restaurant good?
* **この服は似合いますか？** (Kono fuku wa niaimasu ka?) - Does this outfit suit me?
* **今日はどこに行きたいですか？** (Kyou wa doko ni ikitai desu ka?) - Where would you like to go today?
* **何を食べたいですか？** (Nani o tabetai desu ka?) - What would you like to eat?

**4. Asking for Confirmation**

* **もうわかりましたか？** (Mou wakarimashita ka?) - Do you understand now?
* **大丈夫ですか？** (Daijoubu desu ka?) - Are you okay?
* **準備はできましたか？** (Junbi wa dekita desu ka?) - Are you ready?
* **今から行きますか？** (Ima kara ikimasu ka?) - Are you leaving now?
* **決心しましたか？** (Ketsushin shimashita ka?) - Have you made up your mind?

**Additional Tips:**

* Use the particle **か** (ka) at the end of a sentence to make it a question.
* Intonate the last syllable of the sentence slightly higher to indicate that it is a question.
* Be polite when asking questions, especially to people you don't know well.
* Use appropriate vocabulary and grammar depending on the situation.
* Don't be afraid to ask for clarification if you don't understand something.

* **私たちは友達です。** (Watashitachi wa tomodachi desu.) - We are friends.

**3. Emphasizing Similarities or Agreements**

* **私もそう思います。** (Watashi mo sou omoimasu.) - I agree with you.
* **彼はハンサムです。私もそう思います。** (Kare wa hansamu desu. Watashi mo sou omoimasu.) - He is handsome. I agree.
* **この料理はおいしいです。私もそう思います。** (Kono ryori wa oishii desu. Watashi mo sou omoimasu.) - This food is delicious. I agree.
* **日本は住みやすい国です。私もそう思います。** (Nihon wa sumiyasui kuni desu. Watashi mo sou omoimasu.) - Japan is a livable country. I agree.
* **この映画は感動的です。私もそう思います。** (Kono eiga wa kandōteki desu. Watashi mo sou omoimasu.) - This movie is moving. I agree.

**4. Expressing Inclusiveness or Togetherness**

* **みんなで公園に行きましょう。** (Minna de kōen ni ikimashou.) - Let's go to the park together.
* **一緒に食事をしましょう。** (Issho ni shokuji o shimashou.) - Let's eat together.
* **このプロジェクトを一緒にやりましょう。** (Kono purojekuto o issho ni yarimashou.) - Let's work on this project together.
* **みんなで協力すれば、何でもできる。** (Minna de kyōryoku sureba, nandemo dekiru.) - If we all cooperate, we can do anything.
* **一人じゃ寂しいから、一緒に遊ぼう。** (Hitorijanishinai kara, issho ni asobō.) - I'm lonely by myself, so let's play together.

**5. Adding Emphasis or Reinforcing a Point**

* **この問題は重要です。しかも、急ぎです。** (Kono mondai wa jūyō desu. Shikamo, isogidesu.) - This issue is important. Moreover, it's urgent.
* **彼は優秀な学生です。しかも、性格もいいです。** (Kare wa yūshū na gakusei desu. Shikamo, seikaku mo ii desu.) - He is an excellent student. What's more, he has a great personality.
