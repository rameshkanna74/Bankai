## Examples of using "…すぎます" to express something that is excessive or beyond its limit:

**With verbs:**

1. **話しすぎは 良くない よいです。** (Hanashi sugiru wa yoku nai desu.) - Talking too much is not good.

2. **食べすぎは 体からだに 良くない よいです。** (Tabesugiru wa karada ni yoku nai desu.) - Eating too much is not good for your health.

3. **遊びすぎは 勉強べんきょうに 良くない よいです。** (Asobisugiru wa benkyō ni yoku nai desu.) - Playing too much is not good for your studies.

**With i-adjectives:**

1. **この服ふくは 高たかすぎます。** (Kono fuku wa takasugimasu.) - This dress is too expensive.

2. **この映画えいがは 長ながすぎます。** (Kono eiga wa nagasugimasu.) - This movie is too long.

3. **この問題は 難むずかしすぎます。** (Kono mondai wa muzukashiisugimasu.) - This problem is too difficult.

**With na-adjectives:**

1. **この部屋へやは 広ひろすぎます。** (Kono heya wa hirosugimasu.) - This room is too big.

2. **この車は 新あたらしすぎます。** (Kono kuruma wa atarashiisugimasu.) - This car is too new.

3. **この本ほんは 難かむずかしいすぎます。** (Kono hon wa muzukashiisugimasu.) - This book is too difficult.

**Additional notes:**

* The phrase "…すぎます" is formed by adding the verb "すぎる" to the plain form of a verb, adjective, or adverb.

* The verb "すぎる" has a negative connotation, indicating that something is excessive or beyond its limit and has a negative consequence.

* The phrase can be used to express a variety of negative outcomes, such as physical harm, financial loss, or social problems.
